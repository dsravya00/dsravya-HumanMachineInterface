Assignment 2 - Leds and Brushbots

"The interface between human and machine is not just about technology, it's about understanding, empathy, and the potential for collaboration to enhance human experience."
Phase 1 - Wiring Process

For Project 1, the process of creating the final piece was both fun and challenging. It involved carefully connecting wires, experimenting with the brushpot, and ultimately assembling everything to create a set of blocks with holes. I used a few LED lights in colors such as yellow, red, blue, green, and white. These holes were then covered with a total of 12 LEDs. The process of connecting each LED to the next was enjoyable, as was designing a 3D model of a LEGO piece using a 3D printer. However, printing with polylactic acid filament was a lengthy process. I used a sequence of 3 LEDs, connecting them in the following format: positive wire, negative wire, positive wire, negative wire, positive wire, and negative wire, with the final connection going to a resistor. I had used 13 LED lights and used 6 resistor to connect to the small breadboard.

Phase 2 - Brushbot working

For my brushpot, it was my first time creating a motion-based object. Looking at the final piece, I used a simple brushpot with a hat on top. By incorporating a motor and a brush at the front on top, and combining everything together, it was exciting to see how it turned out and how it moved around. Below, I’ve included a 20-second clip of the brushpot, featuring the pink hat added on top.

Phase 3 - Lego Pieces

Using the Tinkercad site, I designed a few Lego blocks and then experimented with 3D printing. With these blocks, I created a cube-shaped hut that had a hole in the center for plugging in LED lights. I integrated these components into the Lego wall. While the printing process for the Lego pieces took quite a bit of time, I was pleased with the final result.



Artist Statement

Using Lego pieces, I built a wall structure in class, incorporating LED lights and a Brush Pot. It turned out to be an exciting activity to create and present. Seeing our LEGO blocks illuminated by the LED lights in the dark was a thrilling experience. The process of integrating the LED lights was challenging, but ultimately rewarding. I used 13 LED lights, 13 hole blocks, and added two LEGO pieces created by the 3D printer, inserting them into my blocks. I had used 100 ohm resistor, can see the image of backview how all togther combine use adding in 4 sets of series with added resistor and tunred out 4 parallel circuits with total of 13 LEDs lights were used. Experimenting with a few unique colors, I constructed a wall with the front view illuminated by the lights and adorned with the LEGO pieces. Finally, the brushpot was added to showcase the movement, completing the piece.
