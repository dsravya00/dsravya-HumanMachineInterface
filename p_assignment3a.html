Phase 1 - Micon Background, Software and Prepping Feather

In Phase 1, I soldered wires and connected the Feather 32 microcontroller. I then assembled all the basic components and prepared them to be placed onto a breadboard. I used a Feather W microcontroller, Feather header pins, and a micro USB cable to connect to the Arduino software for coding. I also used 22-gauge braided wires in green, yellow, red, and black colors. For the breadboard setup, I included two LEDs: a green one and a red one. Additionally, I incorporated various components such as a toggle button, a momentary button, a potentiometer, a photocell, a mini speaker, a motor driver, a vibration motor, and a small slide switch (for on/off control). I also connected the Feather board using resistors to ensure proper functionality of the wiring.
